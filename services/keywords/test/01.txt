<div>                            <p>Apple’s <a href="https://thenextweb.com/topic/iphone/" target="_blank" title="Read more about iPhone.">iPhone</a> X is due to be revealed later today, but it’s apparently already started to hit the benchmarks. Shocker: it’s fast. Like, insanely fast.</p><p>A mysterious <a href="https://browser.geekbench.com/v4/cpu/3967901" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">‘iPhone10,5</a>‘ showed up on Geekbench’s catalog today, and assuming it’s not somehow a false entry, it’s posting speeds that are way faster than anything <a href="https://thenextweb.com/topic/android/" target="_blank" title="Read more about Android.">Android</a>’s current generation has to offer: a 4,095 on the single core test, and 9959 on the multi-core one.</p><div></div><p>For comparison, a quick search of the most powerful Android devices shows they achieve about <em>half</em> of those numbers:</p><ul><li>Samsung S8 :  2024 single, 6279 multi</li><li>OnePlus 5: 1932 single, 6495 multi</li><li>Huawei P10: 1865 single, 5973 multi</li></ul><blockquote data-width="500"><p lang="en" dir="ltr"><a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/iPhoneX?src=hash" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">#iPhoneX</a> spotted on GFXBench.<a href="https://t.co/lSPKKfAX5i" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">https://t.co/lSPKKfAX5i</a><a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/AppleEvent?src=hash" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">#AppleEvent</a> <a href="https://t.co/CHTy9TLf87" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">pic.twitter.com/CHTy9TLf87</a></p><p>— Kid Pool (@KidPoolDead) <a href="https://twitter.com/KidPoolDead/status/907613979735764994" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">September 12, 2017</a></p></blockquote><p></p><p>But it even matches or surpasses devices in ostensibly more powerful categories, even some of Apple’s own tablets and laptops. For example:</p><ul><li><a href="https://thenextweb.com/topic/ipad/" target="_blank" title="Read more about iPad.">iPad</a> Pro 10.5-inch: 3558 single, 9133 multi</li><li><a href="https://thenextweb.com/topic/macbook/" target="_blank" title="Read more about MacBook.">MacBook</a> Air (2017): 3126 single, 5935 multi</li><li><a href="https://thenextweb.com/topic/macbook-pro/" target="_blank" title="Read more about MacBook Pro.">MacBook Pro</a> 13-inch (2017 Core i5): 4342 single, 9194 multi</li></ul><p>Of course, it also far surpasses last year’s <a href="https://thenextweb.com/topic/iphone-7/" target="_blank" title="Read more about iPhone 7.">iPhone 7</a>, which scored a 1825 on the single core test and a 3118 on the multi-core one.</p><p>Granted, benchmarks aren’t everything, but it’s not like we expect the iPhone X to perform <em>poorly</em> despite good benchmarks. This test also seems to confirm <a href="http://appleinsider.com/articles/17/09/10/a11-fusion-in-iphone-x-appears-to-be-a-six-core-processor-according-to-ios-11-leak" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">recent leaks</a> that Apple<a href="https://index.co/company/Apple" data-index="" target="_blank"></a> is increasing the core count from two to six with a new A11 chip. Two of the cores are meant to do the bulk of intensive processing, while the other four are high efficiency cores dedicated to low-power tasks.</p><p>So yeah, Apple’s apparently built a smartphone that’s faster than a 2017 laptop, at least by GeekBench’s metrics.
